FESTIVALS

Makar Sankrati

                
    1. Ahmedabad, Gujarat

        Location: Ahmedabad, Gujarat
        Speciality: The city hosts the International Kite Festival, where the sky fills with colorful kites from across the world. Local foods like undhiyu and chikki add to the festive spirit.
        Best Months: January (around January 14th for Makar Sankranti)
        
        
   2. Jaipur, Rajasthan

        Location: Jaipur, Rajasthan
        Speciality: Makar Sankranti in Jaipur is marked by kite flying competitions, rooftop gatherings, and traditional Rajasthani festivities. The city’s pink skyline creates a vibrant backdrop for the celebration.
        Best Months: January (around January 14th)
        
        
   3. Haridwar, Uttarakhand

        Location: Haridwar, Uttarakhand
        Speciality: In Haridwar, pilgrims gather on Makar Sankranti to take a holy dip in the Ganges River, believing it purifies the soul. The atmosphere is spiritual and culturally rich.
        Best Months: January (around January 14th)
        
        
   4. Varanasi, Uttar Pradesh

        Location: Varanasi, Uttar Pradesh
        Speciality: Known for its holy ghats, Varanasi sees thousands of devotees who take a dip in the Ganges on Makar Sankranti. The city lights up with traditional rituals, boat rides, and kite flying.
        Best Months: January (around January 14th)
        
        
   5. Nashik, Maharashtra

        Location: Nashik, Maharashtra
        Speciality: Nashik’s Makar Sankranti celebrations are unique, with rituals on the Godavari River, kite flying, and cultural events, making it a great place to experience the festival in a spiritual setting.
        Best Months: January (around January 14th)

Lohri

                
    1. Amritsar, Punjab

        Location: Amritsar, Punjab
        Speciality: Known for its grand Lohri celebrations, Amritsar sees bonfires, traditional Punjabi music, dancing, and local delicacies. The festive spirit around the Golden Temple enhances the experience.
        Best Months: January (around January 13th for Lohri)
        
        
   2. Ludhiana, Punjab

        Location: Ludhiana, Punjab
        Speciality: In Ludhiana, Lohri is celebrated with large gatherings, folk music, Bhangra and Gidda dances, and delicious treats like jaggery and sesame sweets. The whole city buzzes with excitement.
        Best Months: January (around January 13th)
        
        
    3. Chandigarh, Punjab and Haryana

        Location: Chandigarh, Union Territory and capital of Punjab and Haryana
        Speciality: Chandigarh combines traditional and modern Lohri festivities, with cultural performances, bonfire parties, and feasts, making it a lively destination for Lohri celebrations.
        Best Months: January (around January 13th)
        
        
    4. Jalandhar, Punjab

        Location: Jalandhar, Punjab
        Speciality: Jalandhar celebrates Lohri with enthusiasm, offering community events, Bhangra performances, and traditional Punjabi food. The warmth of the bonfires and folk music fills the city with joy.
        Best Months: January (around January 13th)
        
        
     5. Delhi

        Location: Delhi
        Speciality: Delhi hosts grand Lohri events, including bonfires, dance performances, and celebrations in Punjabi communities. The festival is marked by sweets, Punjabi dishes, and the city’s cultural diversity.
        Best Months: January (around January 13th)

Rath Yatra

                
   1. Puri, Odisha

        Location: Puri, Odisha
        Speciality: Known for the grandest Rath Yatra in India, the Puri Rath Yatra features massive chariots carrying Lord Jagannath, Balabhadra, and Subhadra. The atmosphere is filled with devotion, music, and a large gathering of devotees.
        Best Months: June to July (during the Rath Yatra festival)
        
        
    2. Ahmedabad, Gujarat

        Location: Ahmedabad, Gujarat
        Speciality: The Rath Yatra in Ahmedabad is celebrated with grand processions, featuring beautifully decorated chariots, music, and a festive spirit as they pass through the city’s major roads.
        Best Months: June to July (during the Rath Yatra festival)
        
        
   3. Kolkata, West Bengal

        Location: Kolkata, West Bengal
        Speciality: Kolkata’s ISKCON Rath Yatra features grand chariots and cultural events, where thousands of devotees participate in the chariot procession and offer prayers, creating a spiritually uplifting experience.
        Best Months: June to July (during the Rath Yatra festival)
        
        
   4. Jagannath Temple, Hyderabad, Telangana

        Location: Hyderabad, Telangana
        Speciality: Hyderabad’s Jagannath Temple organizes a Rath Yatra in the city, attracting devotees who come to see the grand procession, which is accompanied by vibrant celebrations and cultural activities.
        Best Months: June to July (during the Rath Yatra festival)
        
        
   5. Mayapur, West Bengal

        Location: Mayapur, West Bengal
        Speciality: Home to the ISKCON headquarters, Mayapur’s Rath Yatra features a spiritual celebration, with chariot processions, devotional songs, and large gatherings of both devotees and visitors.
        Best Months: June to July (during the Rath Yatra festival)

Navaratri

                
   1. Ahmedabad, Gujarat

        Location: Ahmedabad, Gujarat
        Speciality: Known for the famous Garba and Dandiya Raas dances, Ahmedabad’s Navaratri celebrations include large gatherings with traditional attire, colorful lights, and energetic music. It’s a lively and vibrant experience, with festivities lasting all night.
        
        
        
   2. Vadodara, Gujarat

        Location: Vadodara, Gujarat
        Speciality: Vadodara hosts some of the largest Garba events in India, with elaborate decorations, music, and dance. The Navlakhi Grounds and United Way Garba are popular venues where thousands gather to celebrate.
     
        
        
   3. Kolkata, West Bengal

        Location: Kolkata, West Bengal
        Speciality: In Kolkata, Navaratri coincides with Durga Puja, a grand celebration where artistic idols of Goddess Durga are displayed in beautifully crafted pandals. The entire city lights up with music, cultural performances, and devotional fervor.
        
        
        
   4. Mumbai, Maharashtra

        Location: Mumbai, Maharashtra
        Speciality: Mumbai celebrates Navaratri with elaborate Garba and Dandiya nights, especially in areas like Juhu and Thane. The city also has beautiful pandals, including the famous Lalbaugcha Raja, where people gather for blessings and festivities.
        
        
        
   5. Mysore, Karnataka

        Location: Mysore, Karnataka
        Speciality: Mysore celebrates Navaratri as Dasara, with grand processions, traditional performances, and illumination of the Mysore Palace. The ten-day celebration culminates in a royal procession, adding a unique cultural experience.

Ganesh Chaturthi

                
     1. Mumbai, Maharashtra

        Location: Mumbai, Maharashtra
        Speciality: Known for the grandest Ganesh Chaturthi celebrations in India, Mumbai’s festivities feature giant idols, colorful processions, and community events. The immersion ceremony at Girgaum Chowpatty is a mesmerizing experience.
       
        
        
    2. Pune, Maharashtra

        Location: Pune, Maharashtra
        Speciality: Pune celebrates Ganesh Chaturthi with traditional fervor and spectacular decorations at places like Dagdusheth Halwai Ganpati Temple. The city’s festivities include cultural programs and processions.
       
        
        
    3. Hyderabad, Telangana

        Location: Hyderabad, Telangana
        Speciality: Known for its unique and large idols, Hyderabad’s Ganesh Chaturthi celebrations are marked by community gatherings, colorful pandals, and a grand immersion ceremony at Hussain Sagar Lake.
       
        
        
    4. Bengaluru, Karnataka

        Location: Bengaluru, Karnataka
        Speciality: Bengaluru celebrates Ganesh Chaturthi with eco-friendly idols and a mix of traditional and modern festivities, including community gatherings, music, and dance performances at beautifully decorated pandals.
      
        
        
    5. Chennai, Tamil Nadu

        Location: Chennai, Tamil Nadu
        Speciality: In Chennai, Ganesh Chaturthi is celebrated with religious rituals, processions, and immersion ceremonies. Idols are beautifully decorated, and the celebrations reflect a blend of Tamil culture and devotion.

Diwali

                
    1. Varanasi, Uttar Pradesh

        Location: Varanasi, Uttar Pradesh
        Speciality: Known for its breathtaking “Dev Deepawali” on the ghats of the Ganges, Varanasi is illuminated with thousands of diyas (lamps) and fireworks over the river. The evening Ganga Aarti during Diwali is an unforgettable spiritual experience.
        
        
     2. Jaipur, Rajasthan

        Location: Jaipur, Rajasthan
        Speciality: Jaipur’s famous markets, such as Johari Bazaar and Tripolia Bazaar, are decorated with lights and colors. The entire Pink City glows with lights, and there’s a festive atmosphere with cultural performances and firework displays.
        
        
    3. Amritsar, Punjab

        Location: Amritsar, Punjab
        Speciality: Diwali at the Golden Temple is a grand spectacle, as the temple and its surroundings are illuminated with thousands of lamps. Sikhs and Hindus alike celebrate Diwali here, and the temple’s reflection on the holy tank adds to the beauty.
        
        
    4. Ayodhya, Uttar Pradesh

        Location: Ayodhya, Uttar Pradesh
        Speciality: As the legendary birthplace of Lord Ram, Ayodhya hosts one of the grandest Diwali celebrations, known as “Deepotsav.” Thousands of diyas are lit along the Saryu River, accompanied by cultural programs and a reenactment of Ram’s return.
        
        
     5. Udaipur, Rajasthan

        Location: Udaipur, Rajasthan
        Speciality: Udaipur celebrates Diwali with royal charm, with palaces and lakesides illuminated. The City Palace hosts events, and traditional fireworks over Lake Pichola add to the festive atmosphere, creating a magical Diwali experience.
